02 · Why We Buy, Not Consign

You pushed back on consignment. You were right. So we changed the plan.

The first version of this proposal was built on consignment, selling other people's bags for a cut. You made three points against it, and every one of them holds up. Here they are in your words, with what we are doing instead.

1. Nobody actually consigns.

"Consignment stock is nearly impossible to source. People who own a good bag either keep it or sell it outright. They do not hand it over and wait."

Agreed. So we own the stock. We buy the bag, it is ours, and we never depend on strangers lending us inventory. Supply becomes something we go and get, not something we hope shows up.

2. Consignment squeezes the margin.

"A commission cut is thin. You do all the work of selling and only keep a slice of it."

Agreed. So we keep the full spread. When we own the bag, the entire gap between what we paid and what it sells for is ours. No splitting the upside with an owner who did none of the work.

3. The real profit is in the buy.

"You do not make your money when you sell. You make it when you buy well, under market, from someone motivated."

Agreed. So the whole model is built on sourcing under market. Everything below, the European pipeline, the hard negotiation, the local bargains, exists to buy low. The selling almost takes care of itself once the buying is right.

Consignment is retired. HAUS OF PATINA buys and resells, owns its stock, and keeps everything it earns on the spread. The rest of this plan shows how we do that with almost no capital tied up and almost none of your time.

03 · The Model

Pre-sell the bag, take a deposit, then go and buy it.

This is your idea and it is the heart of the plan. Instead of buying stock and hoping it sells, we sell the bag first and let the buyer's deposit pay for it. Almost no cash of our own is ever at risk, and there is no such thing as unsold stock.

Why this is the whole point.

The buyer's deposit pays for the bag, so almost no capital of ours is ever tied up. There is zero unsold-stock risk, because nothing is bought until it is already sold. And the margin is protected, because the price is agreed before we spend a cent. Capital-light, risk-light, and it scales as fast as we can find buyers.

04 · The Sourcing Edge

A pipeline from Europe, and a net across Cape Town.

A buy and resell house lives or dies on where it buys. HAUS OF PATINA has a genuine structural advantage that competitors cannot copy, plus a steady local lane for bargains.

The main pipeline

The Vevey route

Your mother travels regularly between Vevey, Switzerland and Cape Town. European vintage and pre-loved designer prices sit below South African retail, and as a traveller from outside the EU she can reclaim VAT on eligible purchases, a real saving of roughly 10% to 15% on top.

Bags come over in personal luggage, which means no courier and no international shipping fees on the way in. That is a lower buy price and a lower landed cost than any local rival sourcing the same bag through couriers or agents. This is the engine behind the pre-sell model: buyer commits, we source it on her next trip, it arrives in a suitcase, not on a freight invoice.

The secondary lane

Local Cape Town bargains

Alongside the European pipeline, a small recycling float goes to work locally once cash allows. The AI hunts marketplaces, estate clearances and reseller pages daily for underpriced leather and designer bags, the kind negotiated down to around R900.

We buy low, hold briefly, and flip at roughly three times. This lane needs a little working capital rather than a deposit, so it grows only as profit builds. It keeps stock fresh, feeds content, and turns quick margins between the bigger European pieces.

One honest line on customs.

Bringing bags in personal luggage is a real cost advantage on price and shipping. It is not a way around duty. Bags brought in to resell are commercial goods, so they fall outside a traveller's personal allowance and should be declared to SARS and cleared with the duty and VAT that apply. We build the business to do this properly as volume grows. The edge here is the lower European buy price and no courier, not dodging tax.

05 · How It Runs Itself

The staff are software. The one human bit is outsourced.

Everything a small resale business normally hires people for is run by AI workers, day and night, on Patina's own dedicated accounts. Here is who does what.

AI Worker

Scans marketplaces, estate clearances and European sources every day for underpriced bags. Flags the ones worth buying and works out the resale price before a rand is spent.

AI Worker

The Negotiator

Drafts the hard, polite messages that get a motivated seller from their asking price down to our buy price. The whole model rests on buying well, and this worker does the pushing.

Writes every listing in the HAUS OF PATINA voice, sets pricing from live SA and global comparables, and keeps the catalogue tidy across the website and marketplaces.

AI Worker

Runs the Instagram and TikTok feed on a schedule. Editorial captions, the story of each bag, reels scripts, all on brand, all automatic.

AI Worker

The Concierge

A dedicated WhatsApp brain on its own number answers every enquiry instantly, day or night, holds bags, and collects the 50% deposits and balances that fund the model.

AI Worker

The Bookkeeper

Tracks every buy, every deposit, every sale and every fee, and produces clean monthly numbers so the business is always in order.

The one thing a human must physically do, and how it stays off your plate.

A bag has to be inspected, authenticated and packed. That is the only step software cannot touch, and it does not land on you. It is fully outsourced:

Paid authenticationAn online specialist service verifies each bag (roughly R300 to R900 per item, brand depending), so genuineness is never a guess and never your call.
Courier pickupThe Courier Guy collects and delivers nationally (small parcels from about R90 to R150). No trips, no queues.
A part-time VAAn affordable SA virtual assistant (about R225 to R350 an hour, a few hours a week) handles the physical intake, checks and packing.
Your world stays completely separate. Patina runs on its own dedicated number, its own Instagram and TikTok, its own WhatsApp brain and its own books. It never touches Dean's Atlas or any other system. Your total involvement is approving the odd big buy and enjoying the brand you built.

07 · The Competition

Big and boring, or small and unauthenticated. We sit in the gap.

The SA field splits in two. Luxity is the polished giant that treats a bag like a commodity and takes a large cut from sellers. The Instagram resellers are personal but risky, with no authentication and no story. HAUS OF PATINA is the curated, authenticated, story-led house in between, and because we own our stock and source it under market, we control our own margin.
